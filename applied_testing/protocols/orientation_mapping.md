# Orientation Mapping Protocol (Force Compass)

## Objective

To determine whether any measured residual force depends on device orientation.

## Setup

- asymmetric test device
- low-friction platform or scale
- fixed cable routing
- level surface

## Procedure

Run the experiment at the following orientations:

- 0°
- 90°
- 180°
- 270°

At each orientation:

1. Record baseline (power OFF)
2. Apply power (ON)
3. Record motion or force
4. Remove power (OFF)

## Expected Signature

A candidate signal must:

- reverse direction between 0° and 180°
- show reduced or distinct behavior at 90° and 270°
- remain consistent across runs

## Controls

- dummy load (no plates)
- symmetric plate configuration
- cable reposition test

## Notes

Orientation-dependent behavior is the primary discriminator between geometry-linked effects and environmental artifacts.
