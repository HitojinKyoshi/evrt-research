# EVRT Roadmap Notes

## Overview

The EVRT research roadmap illustrates the conceptual and methodological evolution of the Emergent Vacuum Response Theory framework.

The roadmap is intended to organize:
- theoretical development,
- simulation frameworks,
- experimental metrology,
- and operational scaling architectures.

---

## EVRT v1 — Foundational Phase

The foundational phase focused on:
- emergent vacuum concepts,
- asymmetric electromagnetic systems,
- nonequilibrium field structures,
- and phenomenological theoretical exploration.

This stage established the conceptual basis for later experimental formalization.

---

## EVRT v2 — Constraint and Artifact Analysis Phase

The second phase emphasized:
- systematic artifact rejection,
- falsification methodology,
- null-result interpretation,
- uncertainty analysis,
- and conservation-law consistency.

This stage represented a major shift toward scientific restraint and experimental discipline.

---

## EVRT v3 — Canonical Metrology Framework

The current phase focuses on:
- canonical residual-force scaling laws,
- precision metrology workflows,
- Bayesian classification concepts,
- operational parameterization,
- and reproducibility doctrine.

This stage formalizes the framework into a structured residual-force investigation architecture.

---

## Long-Term Goals

Potential future directions include:
- experimentally calibrated uncertainty analysis,
- improved simulation-to-experiment integration,
- refined parameter extraction methodologies,
- and expanded metrology infrastructure.

---

## Scientific Philosophy

The EVRT roadmap prioritizes:
- falsifiability,
- systematic artifact discrimination,
- reproducibility,
- and experimentally bounded inference.

Null results are considered scientifically meaningful and are expected to constrain phenomenological parameter space.

No experimentally verified anomalous-force mechanism is presently claimed.
