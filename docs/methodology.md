# EVRT Methodology Framework

## Methodological Foundations of the EVRT Research Program

---

## Overview

The EVRT (Emergent Vacuum Response Theory) research framework is a constrained phenomenological investigation into whether structured nonequilibrium electromagnetic systems may exhibit experimentally measurable residual effects under precision metrological conditions.

The framework emphasizes:

- falsifiability
- conservation-law consistency
- uncertainty-aware interpretation
- reproducibility-oriented experimentation
- systematic artifact discrimination
- precision residual-force metrology

The EVRT repository is not intended to promote claims of verified antigravity, reactionless propulsion, or experimentally confirmed violations of known physical law.

Instead, the framework is designed as a structured research environment for exploring whether highly controlled nonequilibrium electromagnetic architectures may motivate experimentally testable hypotheses within rigorous uncertainty and diagnostic constraints.

---

# Scientific Position

The EVRT framework adopts a conservative scientific position.

All proposed effects are treated as:

- hypothetical
- phenomenological
- experimentally unverified
- subject to conventional explanations unless rigorously excluded

Null results are considered scientifically meaningful because they:

- constrain parameter space
- establish sensitivity bounds
- improve metrological methodology
- refine experimental architectures
- reduce ambiguity in residual-force interpretation
