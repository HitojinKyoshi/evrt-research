# Research Philosophy

## Scientific Orientation

The EVRT framework is intended as a constrained phenomenological research program focused on experimentally testable nonequilibrium electromagnetic systems.

The framework does not assume verified anomalous propulsion, antigravity, or new fundamental interactions.

Instead, the program emphasizes:
- falsifiability,
- conservation-law consistency,
- uncertainty analysis,
- systematic artifact rejection,
- and reproducible metrology.

---

## Falsifiability

All proposed residual-force hypotheses are expected to remain experimentally testable and subject to null-result constraints.

The framework treats:
- negative results,
- non-detections,
- and failed hypotheses

as scientifically valuable outcomes.

---

## Artifact Rejection

A central principle of the EVRT framework is that candidate residual-force measurements must survive rigorous exclusion of known systematic effects.

Potential artifact sources include:
- thermal drift,
- electrohydrodynamic forces,
- vibration coupling,
- electromagnetic interference,
- and instrumentation limitations.

---

## Precision Metrology

The framework prioritizes:
- controlled experimentation,
- geometry reversal,
- vacuum persistence testing,
- dummy-load analysis,
- and uncertainty-aware interpretation.

The objective is not to maximize anomalous claims, but rather to maximize experimental rigor.

---

## Reproducibility

Reproducibility is considered essential.

The framework encourages:
- transparent methodology,
- structured experimental logging,
- open-source documentation,
- and accessible analysis workflows.

---

## Phenomenological Approach

Current models are phenomenological rather than first-principles derivations.

The framework is intended primarily as:
- a structured experimental architecture,
- a metrology framework,
- and a residual-force classification methodology.

---

## Current Status

No experimentally verified anomalous-force effect has been established within the EVRT framework.

Current research remains exploratory and focuses primarily on:
- operational scaling structures,
- artifact discrimination,
- and experimentally bounded inference.
