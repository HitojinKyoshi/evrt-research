# Uncertainty Budget Framework

## Overview

This document summarizes anticipated uncertainty sources relevant to precision residual-force investigations in coherent nonequilibrium electromagnetic systems.

The framework is intended to support:
- falsifiability,
- artifact rejection,
- parameter bounding,
- and reproducible metrology workflows.

No experimentally verified anomalous-force signal is presently claimed.

---

## Primary Uncertainty Sources

### Thermal Drift
Potential torsion-arm drift caused by:
- Joule heating,
- radiative asymmetry,
- convection,
- and thermal expansion.

Mitigation strategies:
- thermal stabilization,
- geometry reversal,
- dummy-load controls,
- delayed-response analysis.

---

### Electrohydrodynamic Effects
Possible residual forces arising from:
- ion wind,
- corona discharge,
- charged airflow,
- dielectric polarization.

Mitigation strategies:
- vacuum operation,
- plasma monitoring,
- field shielding,
- pressure sweeps.

---

### Mechanical Vibrations
Potential coupling from:
- bench vibration,
- cable motion,
- acoustic resonance,
- environmental disturbances.

Mitigation strategies:
- vibration isolation,
- laser stability monitoring,
- suspended torsion systems,
- resonance characterization.

---

### Electromagnetic Coupling
Possible environmental interactions from:
- RF leakage,
- Lorentz forces,
- magnetic-field asymmetry,
- grounding pathways.

Mitigation strategies:
- dummy-load testing,
- shielded operation,
- current-path characterization,
- magnetic null testing.

---

## Null-Result Interpretation

Null results are considered scientifically meaningful and are expected to constrain effective phenomenological response coefficients and operational parameter space.

---

## Current Status

This uncertainty framework remains qualitative and conceptual pending experimentally calibrated measurements and systematic instrument characterization.
