# Artifact Probability Model

## Overview

This document outlines a conceptual probabilistic framework for estimating whether candidate residual-force measurements in coherent nonequilibrium electromagnetic systems are more likely attributable to known artifacts or unexplained residual effects.

The framework is exploratory and phenomenological only.

No experimentally verified anomalous-force mechanism is presently claimed.

---

## Conceptual Framework

Observed measurements are interpreted as competing probabilistic hypotheses.

For a measured residual response \(R\), the framework estimates:

\[
P(H_i | R)
\]

where:

- \(H_i\) represents a candidate hypothesis,
- \(R\) represents the observed measurement,
- and \(P(H_i | R)\) represents the posterior probability of the hypothesis given the measurement.

---

## Candidate Hypotheses

Potential hypotheses include:

| Hypothesis | Description |
|---|---|
| \(H_T\) | Thermal drift or thermal expansion |
| \(H_V\) | Mechanical vibration coupling |
| \(H_E\) | Electrohydrodynamic or plasma effects |
| \(H_M\) | Electromagnetic interference or Lorentz coupling |
| \(H_C\) | Calibration or instrumentation error |
| \(H_R\) | Candidate unexplained residual effect |

---

## Bayesian Updating

Posterior probabilities may be updated using additional diagnostic evidence including:

- geometry reversal consistency,
- vacuum persistence,
- resonance dependence,
- phase coherence,
- thermal lag behavior,
- dummy-load response,
- environmental monitoring,
- and scaling-law agreement.

The framework is intended to favor conventional explanations unless multiple independent diagnostic criteria remain inconsistent with known artifacts.

---

## Example Interpretation

A hypothetical posterior distribution might appear as:

| Hypothesis | Probability |
|---|---|
| Thermal artifact | 0.52 |
| Mechanical vibration | 0.21 |
| Electromagnetic coupling | 0.17 |
| Candidate residual | 0.10 |

In this example, the framework would interpret the measurement as overwhelmingly likely to arise from conventional systematic effects.

---

## Scientific Philosophy

The EVRT framework assumes that most candidate residual-force measurements will ultimately be attributable to conventional effects unless robust experimental controls demonstrate otherwise.

The probability model is therefore designed primarily as:
- an artifact discrimination tool,
- an uncertainty-management framework,
- and a falsification-support methodology.

---

## Current Status

The present model is conceptual and qualitative.

Future development may incorporate:
- experimentally calibrated likelihood functions,
- empirical uncertainty distributions,
- Monte Carlo uncertainty propagation,
- and adaptive Bayesian updating using real experimental datasets.
